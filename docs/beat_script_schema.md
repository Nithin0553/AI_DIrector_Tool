# Beat Script Schema

This document defines the structure of a Beat Script generated by the AI Director tool.

A Beat Script is a machine-readable representation of cinematic instructions that guide the Unity cutscene engine.

Each beat represents a small moment in the scene, typically lasting between 1–5 seconds.

---

# Beat Structure

Each beat contains the following fields:

- scene_id
- beat_id
- speaker
- dialogue
- action
- emotion
- intent
- shot_type
- camera_angle
- camera_movement
- focus_target
- secondary_target
- duration
- transition

---

# Field Descriptions

### scene_id
Unique identifier for the scene.

Example:
1

---

### beat_id
Sequential identifier for each beat within a scene.

Example:
3

---

### speaker
The character speaking during the beat.

Example:
COOPER

---

### dialogue
The dialogue spoken by the character.

Example:
"Sorry. Go back to sleep."

---

### action
Optional action occurring during the beat.

Example:
Murph looks scared.

---

### emotion
The emotional tone detected in the beat.

Example values:

- calm
- fear
- anger
- curiosity

---

### intent
The narrative intention of the line.

Examples:

- reassure
- question
- confront
- explain

---

### shot_type
Camera framing type.

Examples:

- medium_shot
- close_up
- over_shoulder

---

### camera_angle
Vertical angle of the camera.

Examples:

- eye_level
- low_angle
- high_angle

---

### camera_movement
Camera motion during the shot.

Examples:

- static
- pan
- push_in
- dolly_in

---

### focus_target
Primary character or object being filmed.

Example:
COOPER

---

### secondary_target
Secondary subject in the frame.

Example:
MURPH

---

### duration
Length of the shot in seconds.

Example:
2.4

---

### transition
How the shot transitions to the next shot.

Examples:

- cut
- dissolve
- fade

---
